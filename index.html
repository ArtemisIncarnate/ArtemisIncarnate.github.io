URBAN PARKS AND GREENSPACES
Increasing green spaces in urban areas improves health, mental wellness,
air quality, and provides a place for community to gather. However,
many studies show that the development and distribution of these spaces
is disproportionate and often benefits predominately White and/or more
affluent communities, creating environmental justice issues.


BREC's mission is to contribute to a healthier, more vibrant community
by providing exceptional parks, open spaces and recreational experiences
for all of East Baton Rouge Parish.

BREC's vision is to provide an extraordinary system of parks, open spaces,
and facilities that engages the Parish's unique natural and cultural landscape
to enrich Parish life by providing diverse and memorable recreation experience.

BREC has more than 180 parks including a unique mix of facilities, which
mirror the history and rich natural resources in the region; including a
state-of-the-art observatory, a swamp nature center and conservation areas,
a performing arts theatre, an equestrian park, an art gallery, an arboretum,
an accredited zoo, seven golf courses and an extreme sports park with a 30,000-foot
concrete skate park, rock-climbing wall, BMX track and velodrome.

Since 2004, BREC has constructed twelve community parks, five dog parks, a
family-sized Aquatics Center, a growing trails system, fishing ponds,
larger playgrounds and procured two mobile recreation units to serve play
deserts in the area.
http://www.brec.org/

Though parks and public spaces should be easily accessible by all community
members, studies suggest urban greenspaces tend to imporve predominantly
White neighborhoods. As a predominantly Black and White community, segregated
by socioeconomic factors, it is important that the tax-payer-supported
BREC centers and facilities be distributed equiably to aviod environmental
justice issues and gentrification.

Public transportation infrastructure in Baton Rouge is not easily or equitably
accessed for all persons in all areas. Unfortunately, not all BREC parks are
along easy bus routes. For lower income groups who are dependent on the bus
system to enjoy BREC facilities, it is necessary that BREC parks are either
on a direct bus line or a walkable distance.

By using the web map and applications, users and client editors can determine
the level of access to BREC facilities for community members from all
socioeconomic groups and ethinicities. Editors can analyze data pulled from
embedded map/app layers by joining attributes and searching for the nearest
facilities.This information can be used by client editors to propose new
bus routes and times, additional greentrails, improve planning, and
inform policy.
